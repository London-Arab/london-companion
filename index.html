Shopping

London is Europe’s most visited shopping city. Oxford Street alone boasts more than 300 retailers, ranging from high‑street fashion and department stores to designer flagships. Nearby Regent Street and Bond Street offer luxury boutiques and flagship stores, while Westfield London in Shepherd’s Bush contains hundreds of shops and restaurants under one roof. For high‑end shopping and exclusive brands, explore the famous department stores of Knightsbridge such as Harrods and Harvey Nichols.

Restaurants

London’s culinary scene spans Michelin‑starred dining rooms, vibrant street‑food markets and neighbourhood cafés. For halal options, try:

  • Ramo Ramen in Soho – a halal‑certified ramen bar blending Filipino and Japanese flavours; popular dishes include miso‑baked shrimp dumplings and creative broths.
  • Etles Uyghur Restaurant in Walthamstow – run by a husband‑and‑wife team, serving hand‑pulled noodles, lamb skewers and other authentic Uyghur dishes.
  • Marvee’s Food Shop in Paddington – a Caribbean restaurant where everything is halal, from hearty goat curry to creamy mac and cheese.
  • Gymkhana in Mayfair – an elegant Indian restaurant with two Michelin stars offering tandoori meats, biryanis and kebabs.

For non‑halal dining, London offers world cuisines from British gastropubs and European brasseries to Asian fusion and modern street food. You’ll also find Lebanese and Turkish restaurants along Edgware Road and Bayswater.

Historical Sites

Explore London’s rich history, from Roman beginnings to Victorian splendour. The Tower of London traces over 900 years of royal history and is home to the Crown Jewels. Tour Buckingham Palace, the official residence of the monarch, and discover the stories of Westminster Abbey, where monarchs have been crowned for centuries. Don’t miss St Paul’s Cathedral with its baroque dome, Shakespeare’s Globe theatre and the British Museum, which houses artefacts from around the world. Many of these sites offer guided tours that bring the past to life.

Parks

London is home to eight Royal Parks—Hyde Park, Kensington Gardens, St James’s Park, Green Park, Regent’s Park, Greenwich Park, Bushy Park and Richmond Park—which offer peaceful green spaces amid the bustling city. Enjoy boating on the Serpentine in Hyde Park, watch pelicans being fed in St James’s Park or explore the rose gardens of Regent’s Park. Deer roam freely in Richmond Park, while Greenwich Park provides panoramic views across the River Thames. These parks include playgrounds, cafés and scenic paths for families and nature lovers.

Attractions

London’s attractions are among the most iconic in the world. Uncover centuries of royal history and see the Crown Jewels at the Tower of London or watch the Changing of the Guard at Buckingham Palace. Take a ride on the London Eye for panoramic views of the skyline and explore the magnificent architecture of Westminster Abbey. Don’t miss the Warner Bros. Studio Tour – The Making of Harry Potter, where you can step onto the sets of the film series. The city also boasts world‑class museums such as the British Museum, Tate Modern and the Victoria and Albert Museum, many of which are free to enter.

Rooftop Experiences

For spectacular views of London’s skyline, visit rooftop venues such as the Sky Garden, London’s highest public garden with landscaped terraces and a 360‑degree viewing platform—free but bookable in advance. Savour cocktails and fusion dishes at Sushisamba City, which boasts one of the highest outdoor terraces in Europe and panoramic views across the City. Aviary London offers stylish interiors, creative cocktails and a spacious terrace overlooking the capital; in winter, cosy igloos provide al‑fresco dining year‑round. Elsewhere, rooftop bars along South Bank and the City combine drinks and dining with breathtaking vistas.

Shisha Cafes

London has a variety of shisha lounges where you can unwind. In Knightsbridge, 17 on Sloane features a heated terrace with classic and signature shisha blends, located just off Sloane Street only moments from Harrods. It opens daily from 6 pm until 1 am and prides itself on attentive service and pairing shisha with drinks and mocktails. Along Edgware Road and in Westminster you’ll also find Middle Eastern cafés and lounges serving shisha alongside Arabic coffee and snacks, all within a 30‑minute drive from Harrods.
